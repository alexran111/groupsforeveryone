General information about our groups

Los Altos, CA

Approach and Goals

Groups meet once a week for 90 minute sessions. You will have two assessment sessions prior to joining a group with the therapist to evaluate your needs and find the best matching group.

Approach: The style of these psychotherapy groups is interpersonally oriented, using Mindfulness and CBT skills. We focus on individual goals while growing self-awareness and interpersonal effectiveness. Participants gain insight into their relationship styles and explore and increase their self-efficacy.

Goals:

  • Increased ability to handle stressful situations
  • Staying mindful in the midst of anxiety
  • Better understand one's own automatic reactions and learn to find new, healthier coping skills

Home

"Groups for everyone" is an initiative in Los Altos, CA, currently led by Ildiko Ran, MFT Registered Intern to povide the community with the much needed resource of offering therapy and support groups for individuals who are looking for a safe, genuine way to find and nurture their capacity to live, love, and work in a balanced way, and feeling comfortable about themselves.

We invite you to browse through our offerings and contact us if you are interested in our existing groups. If you don't find what you are looking for, we promise our best effort to create a group that best fits your needs.

We think outside the box.

  • We Design

    We pride ourselves in providing you with the optimal group experience.

  • We Create

    We create and maintain small groups where you will feel safe, heard, and able to thrive.

  • We Nurture

    We provide a place to improve and optimize your social skills. We humans need groups to thrive.

  • We Collaborate

    We maintain ties with local therapists to provide the best referral network. We know who is the best fit for your individual and group needs.

About

GROUPS

Ildiko Ran, MFT Intern specializes in process group therapy and psychodynamic individual therapy. Having worked at high school settings, a hospital program for those affected by perinatal mood disorders, and at a non-profit clinic providing parenting classes for high conflict divorcing families.

Having lived in various cultures, being part of a diverse family system, having raised her children and stepchildren adds to Ildiko's perspective on life.
